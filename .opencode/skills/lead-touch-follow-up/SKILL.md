# Lead Touch Follow-Up

Use this skill to decide whether a contact deserves a follow-up, what kind of follow-up is appropriate, and what must be captured before the next touch.

## Classify First

Classify each contact before drafting:

1. `active opportunity`: concrete role/project, current exchange, or interview path.
2. `waiting`: prior reply sent; no new information.
3. `needs clarification`: missing client, rate, model, remote, process, or authorization terms.
4. `relationship-protected`: active client, sensitive partner, or closed/lost account.
5. `suppressed`: low fit, duplicate, vague sourcing, spam, or policy conflict.

Do not draft until classification is complete.

## Follow-Up Cadence

Use conservative timing by default:

- Same day: only for scheduling, hot recruiter/client movement, interview logistics, or corrections.
- 2-3 business days: active recruiter or hiring-manager thread with no response.
- 5-7 business days: application follow-up where a real contact exists.
- No follow-up: no named contact, no concrete role, generic ATS acknowledgement, or low-fit automated message.

## Message Shapes

### Clarifying recruiter screen

Ask for the missing gates before a call or submission:

`Thanks for reaching out. Before we schedule or submit, can you confirm the end client or hiring group, role scope, W2/1099/C2C/direct-hire model, compensation or rate range, remote/hybrid/onsite expectations, interview timeline, and any right-to-represent or exclusivity terms?`

### Warm hiring-manager follow-up

Use only when there is a credible contact path:

`I applied for [role] and wanted to add one concise note on fit: [specific evidence]. If useful, I would be glad to discuss [specific business problem] and how I would approach the first 30-60 days.`

### Waiting-thread bump

Use when a prior reply is unanswered:

`Following up on the note below. I am still interested if the role is active, especially if [key condition] is still true. Happy to discuss next steps or send anything else helpful.`

### Decline / routing preference

Use when the role fails constraints but the contact may be useful:

`This specific role is not a fit because [constraint]. Please keep me in mind for [target roles] that are [remote/comp/model constraints] and include client, process, and compensation details.`

## Do Not Over-Message

Suppress follow-up when:

- The last touch was too recent.
- There is no named person.
- The thread is only an ATS acknowledgement.
- The opportunity is low fit or below threshold.
- The contact already answered and the next action belongs to the candidate.
- A duplicate channel would create confusion.

## Required Output

Before sending, produce:

`Contact | Opportunity | Classification | Draft | Missing gates | Send / hold | Follow-up date | Evidence`

## Human Approval Gates

Require human approval before:

- Sending a message externally.
- Attaching a resume or private document.
- Agreeing to representation, exclusivity, background-check, offer, or start-date terms.
- Changing compensation, relocation, travel, onsite, or sponsorship posture.
- Contacting an active client or sensitive relationship.

## Quality Bar

Every follow-up should be short, specific, and state-changing. If the message does not clarify terms, advance scheduling, add differentiated evidence, or close the loop, do not send it.
